Equality, Diversity, and Inclusion

Equality, Diversity, and Inclusion at Hiqual UK

Hiqual UK is dedicated to fostering a culture of equality, diversity, and inclusion among our workforce, learners, and stakeholders. We firmly believe in providing fair treatment and ensuring that no individual is subjected to unlawful discrimination. We strive to create an inclusive environment that respects and values individuals from all backgrounds.

We are committed to upholding the principles set forth in the Equality Act 2010, which outlines the legal duties and protections against discrimination in the UK. This act prohibits discrimination based on various protected characteristics, including:

  1. Age
  2. Gender reassignment
  3. Marriage and civil partnership
  4. Pregnancy and maternity
  5. Race (including color, nationality, ethnic or national)
  6. Religion or belief (including lack of belief)
  7. Sex
  8. Sexual orientation
  9. Disability

We expect all our stakeholders, including Learning Partners, to adhere to their own equal opportunities policies and procedures. For Learning Partners operating in the UK, it is essential that their policies are in accordance with the Equality Act 2010. Learning Partners outside the UK should align their policies with local legislation.

We take all complaints seriously and have established procedures to address any concerns related to equality, diversity, and inclusion. Our complaint resolution process ensures fair and timely investigation of all matters raised.

If you wish to raise a complaint, please contact our designated equality officer who will guide you through the process and ensure your concerns are addressed appropriately.

Unfair treatment in any form is not tolerated at HiQual UK. We are committed to providing a safe and inclusive environment where all individuals are treated with dignity and respect.

If you experience or witness unfair treatment, we encourage you to report it immediately. All reports will be investigated thoroughly and appropriate action will be taken to address any misconduct.
